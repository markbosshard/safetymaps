Is Cali safe for travelers?

AvoidExercise Increased Caution US State Dept — Colombia

Cali is rated high-risk by major travel safety sources. The US State Dept advises "Exercise Increased Caution" for Colombia due to crime, civil unrest, and terrorism. Extra precautions are strongly recommended; consult the sources listed below before travel.

What the sources say

Cali is rated Avoid. Colombia's third city has very high violent crime driven by gang warfare, narco-trafficking, and competition between criminal groups. All three advisories flag Cali under Colombia's elevated risk rating; the US advisory issues specific Cali guidance within its Colombia page.

Robbery is extremely common across the city including in tourist areas (San Antonio, Granada). The eastern comunas (Aguablanca district) are effectively no-go. Homicide rates are among the highest in Colombia. Even the "safer" west side neighbourhoods carry Caution-level risk.

If visiting Cali — primarily for the salsa culture — stay in Ciudad Jardín or Granada, use vetted transport only, never walk after dark, and join organised salsa tours rather than independently bar-hopping. Minimise time outside the hotel and organised venues. This requires significantly higher vigilance than Medellín or Bogotá.

Common questions

Is Cali safe for tourists?

Cali is considered high-risk by major travel authorities. Travellers should thoroughly research current conditions, stay in vetted accommodations, use private transport, and minimise unnecessary movement, especially after dark.

Is Cali safe at night?

Cali is considered high-risk at night. Minimise unnecessary travel after dark, use trusted private transport, and stay in secure, well-reviewed accommodations.

What are the main safety risks for visitors to Cali?

Cali sees higher rates of robbery, vehicle theft, and in some areas more serious incidents. Official sources highlight crime, civil unrest, and terrorism as key concerns in Colombia.
